# Creative UI Development with AI (CUDAI)
### Course Outline

## Overview

**Creative UI Development with AI (CUDAI)** is a premium, hands-on course that teaches designers, founders, and builders to ship award-winning websites *and* polished product interfaces using AI coding tools — not just AI image generators.

Most AI design courses stop at the mockup. CUDAI takes you all the way to the live, animated, interactive build. You'll learn to direct AI coding agents (Claude Code, Antigravity) and orchestrate modern web tech — WebGL, GSAP, Framer Motion, Lenis — to build the kind of work that wins Awwwards and the kind of product UI that feels genuinely premium, without writing every line by hand.

You'll also learn to build your own custom tools — turning AI from something you rent into something you shape around your own workflow.

**The goal:** design with code, at the speed of AI, with output that's distinctly yours.

## Course Duration

**Weeks 1–3: Live Classes**
- 2–3 sessions per week
- 7 total parts

**Week 4: Final Project Presentations**
- Project reviews
- Feedback sessions
- Portfolio recommendations

---

## Part 1: The New Build Stack

### Topics Covered
- From mockup-maker to site-shipper: why "design" and "development" just merged
- The evolution of design in the AI era, and why AI isn't replacing great builders
- The difference between generating and building
- Setting up your AI coding environment (Claude Code, Antigravity)
- The agentic workflow: how to brief, review, and direct AI that writes real code
- Essential tools for the modern AI-powered builder

### Learning Outcomes
Students will understand how AI coding agents fit into the design-to-build process and set up a workflow that increases speed without sacrificing quality — for both expressive sites and product interfaces.

### Assignment
Set up your stack and ship a one-section live page.

---

## Part 2: Designing With Taste and Intention

### Topics Covered
- What makes a site feel premium *and* what makes a product feel crafted
- The details that separate "looks fine" from "feels expensive"
- Designing with intention: the reasoning behind every visual choice
- Developing visual judgment
- Building moodboards and references
- Using AI to explore directions you then build, not just render

### Learning Outcomes
Students will learn to direct AI outputs with intention rather than accept generic results, and to recognize the craft that makes interfaces and products feel premium.

### Assignment
Pick a buildable direction for a real-world site — an architecture/interior-design studio, a physical-product demo, or a photography portfolio — and develop it into three distinct design directions.

---

## Part 3: Motion & Interaction Foundations

### Topics Covered
- Where the Awwwards-level magic lives
- GSAP for timeline and scroll-triggered animation
- Lenis for smooth scroll
- Framer Motion for component interactions and product-UI microinteractions
- Generating and refining motion with AI instead of fighting documentation

### Learning Outcomes
Students will learn to add the motion and interaction that elevate a build from static to memorable.

### Assignment
Take your chosen direction from Part 2 and bring it to life — apply motion and interaction to the site you picked.

---

## Part 4: Building Custom Tools

Where everything so far converges. You've learned to direct AI, design with taste and intention, and add motion — now you turn that on *yourself*, building tools shaped around your own workflow.

### Topics Covered
- **Product thinking for tools** — who it's for (often just you), the one job it does well, and ruthless scope-cutting. The hardest part of a tool isn't building it, it's deciding what *not* to build.
- **From repetitive pain to product** — spotting the manual task you keep redoing and turning it into a small app, generator, or utility.
- **Craft applied to utility** — the taste and motion from Parts 2–3, applied to internal tools. "It's just for me" is no excuse for it feeling broken.
- **Architecting with AI** — directing Claude Code / Antigravity to scaffold, structure, and iterate on real working software, not just snippets.
- **Making it reusable** — building something you'll actually open again next week.

### Learning Outcomes
Students will learn to combine everything covered so far into functional, crafted tools — and to apply product thinking to software they build for themselves.

### Assignment
Identify a repetitive task in your own workflow and build a custom tool that solves it — functional, and crafted.

---

## Part 5: WebGL & Immersive Experiences

### Topics Covered
- Adding depth — literally
- Intro to WebGL/Three.js through AI assistance
- Shaders, 3D scenes, and interactive canvases without a graphics-programming background
- Knowing when immersive 3D elevates an experience and when it's overkill

### Learning Outcomes
Students will learn to add immersive, three-dimensional elements to their work using AI assistance.

### Assignment
Add a 3D character to your site using WebGL — for example, a 3D bust generated from your own image.

---

## Part 6: Building Complete Products

### Topics Covered
- End-to-end workflows: idea → design → code → deployed
- Design-to-prototype and design-to-code processes
- AI-assisted iteration and refinement
- Performance and launch preparation
- Tying GSAP, Lenis, Framer Motion, and WebGL into one cohesive experience
- Building complete interactive websites and product UIs

### Case Studies
- Awwwards-winning portfolios & agency sites
- Premium product UIs
- AI-built startup products
- Independent creator success stories

### Learning Outcomes
Students will understand how to move from an idea to a production-ready product experience.

### Assignment
Complete your final capstone project for presentation.

---

## Part 7: Final Words & More Resources

### Topics Covered
- Where to go from here: continuing to grow as an AI-powered builder
- Staying current as tools evolve
- Curated resources: tools, libraries, communities, and references
- Building a body of work and a repeatable personal framework
- Final reflections and parting advice

## Capstone Project

Two deliverables:

1. **The Client Build** — a complete site or product UI built for a client, real or fictional. Concept → design → animated, interactive, deployed build.
2. **Your Custom Tool** — a more ambitious tool built for your own workflow, extending what you made in Part 4 and demonstrating you can shape AI into your own utilities rather than only consuming it.

---

## Final Presentation Week

Students will present:
- Product concept and client brief
- Design process
- AI workflow used
- Final UI screens / live build
- The custom tool they built
- Key lessons learned

Each presentation will receive structured feedback and improvement recommendations.

## Core Promise

Most AI tools help you *generate* a picture of a website.

**This course teaches you to build the real thing** — award-worthy sites, premium product UIs, and your own custom tools — by directing AI to write the code.

Students will learn to combine design thinking, visual taste, and AI-powered code workflows to produce work that is faster, better, and distinctly human.
